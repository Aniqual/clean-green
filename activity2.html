Explanation (‚âà180 words)

The hybrid system integrates solar PV and wind energy to provide reliable power to a rural health clinic. Solar panels convert sunlight to DC electricity while a wind turbine produces power from moving air. Both feed their respective charge controllers, which regulate charging of a shared battery bank. A hybrid inverter converts the stored DC to AC for powering medical loads such as vaccine refrigeration, sterilizers, and diagnostic equipment. During prolonged cloudy or windless periods, an optional diesel generator automatically starts to maintain continuity. Outputs include continuous AC supply and backup capability. Real-world relevance: Hybridization reduces dependency on diesel fuel, lowers operational cost, and ensures uninterrupted health services in remote areas. It also supports sustainable rural electrification, enabling clinics to store medicines safely and operate essential life-saving devices even in grid-deficient regions.
